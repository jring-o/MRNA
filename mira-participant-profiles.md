## Cottage Groupings

You have **22 participants** and **cottages of 3** — which doesn't divide evenly.
The proposal below uses **7 cottages: six of 3 + one of 4**. If a cottage of 4
isn't possible, the obvious split is to move one person from Cottage A into
Cottage G (note at the bottom). The groupings deliberately mix collaborators
who already know each other with people they probably haven't met, so each
cottage has at least one "shared core" plus one or two new bridges.

### Cottage A — Wet-lab cell/biophysics (4)
- **Matt Akamatsu** (UW, DG lab)
- **Kate Lee** (Toronto, condensates)
- **Sean Moore** (McGill, Weber lab — condensates / chromatin)
- **Morgan Sosa** (Quantum Biology Institute — builds lab infra)

*Why:* All four live the wet-lab side. Matt + Kate + Sean overlap on
condensate / microscopy science and span three different career stages
(PI–PI–PhD). Morgan is the "infrastructure for actual humans" voice in the
room and connects the lab life back to the publishing pipeline. This cottage
will spend half the workshop talking about ELNs.

### Cottage B — Discourse Graphs & knowledge-unit theorists (3)
- **Joel Chan** (UMD, DG academic lead)
- **Marc-Antoine Parent** (HyperKnowledge / DG engineer)
- **Paul Reginato** (Homeworld Collective — fiscally sponsors DG; problem-statement framework)

*Why:* The DG inner ring + the field-builder who funds them. Joel and MAP
share the formalism conversation; Paul brings the "what would researchers
actually do with this" pressure-test plus the Homeworld problem-statement
framework as a parallel modular unit.

### Cottage C — Ethnography, ATproto & local-first knowledge networks (3)
