# MIRA Workshop Post-Event Survey — Draft Questions

Draft survey for participants of the MIRA workshop (Deerstone Hideaway, 7–11 June 2026, ~23 participants).
Each question lists its **response type** and the **goal** — what decision or insight it's meant to support.

> **Naming convention:** "**MIRA**" = the schema / standard / community we're building.
> "**the workshop**" / "**the gathering**" = the in-person event being surveyed. The two are
> deliberately kept distinct: some questions measure whether the *standard* advanced, others
> measure whether the *gathering* was good.

**Design notes**
- Target length: ~5–8 minutes (~15–18 questions). Completion rate matters more than coverage.
- **Core / repeatable** questions are marked ⭐ — ask these at *every* MIRA workshop to track trends over time.
- Recommended: anonymous, with an optional name field, so facilitation feedback stays candid.
- Scales are 1–5 unless noted (1 = poor / strongly disagree, 5 = excellent / strongly agree).

---

## Section 1 — Overall Experience

### Q1. Overall, how would you rate the MIRA workshop? ⭐
- **Type:** 1–5
- **Goal:** A single headline satisfaction number that can be tracked across every future workshop. The top-line health metric for the *gathering*.

### Q2. How confident are you that continuing work on MIRA is the right direction — that it will improve research as the schema and ecosystem evolve? ⭐
- **Type:** 1–5 (or 0–10)
- **Goal:** This was a room of people who *already* use the schema, so adoption-intent is moot. The leading indicator for a project built by its own users is *conviction*: does the core believe continuing the work is worth it — that MIRA will actually improve research as the schema and ecosystem evolve? Track it every gathering — rising confidence signals momentum; a dip is an early warning even if everyone keeps showing up.
- **Optional paired item:** "Compared to before the workshop, your confidence is: Higher / About the same / Lower." — measures whether the gathering itself built or eroded conviction (a direct workshop outcome).
- **Optional follow-up (open text):** "What would most increase your confidence in MIRA?" — surfaces what's holding conviction back, and what MIRA needs to deliver to earn it.

### Q2b. How likely are you to keep contributing to MIRA after the workshop — schema PRs, tooling, user stories, graph data, or participating in the community? ⭐
- **Type:** 0–10 (or 1–5)
- **Goal:** A standard and its community live or die on sustained contribution *between* gatherings. Distinguishes passive adopters from active builders and identifies the committed core to follow up with.

### Q2c. How likely are you to bring others into MIRA — introduce a collaborator, lab, or platform to the schema or community? ⭐
- **Type:** 0–10 (or 1–5)
- **Goal:** Advocacy is how the standard spreads beyond the room. Adoption (Q2) grows depth; this grows *reach* — and getting *other* platforms to inherit the schema is MIRA's stated end goal.
- **Optional follow-up (open text):** "Who or what would you introduce?" — seeds the next round of outreach.

### Q3. Did the workshop meet your expectations?
- **Type:** Single choice — Exceeded / Met / Below
- **Goal:** Catches expectation mismatch early. If many say "below," it signals the framing/invitation set wrong expectations even if the gathering was good.

### Q4. In one sentence, what was the single most valuable thing you got out of the MIRA workshop?
- **Type:** Open text
- **Goal:** Surfaces the real value participants perceive (often different from what organizers assume). Great for testimonials and for shaping how the next workshop is pitched.

## Section 2 — Program & Content

### Q5. How valuable was each session type?
- **Type:** Matrix, 1–5 + "didn't attend" — rows: lightning talks · user stories as anchors · the marketplace · daily breakouts · group report-backs · road-to-adoption & commitments · persistent hacking track
- **Goal:** Identifies which formats earned their place and which to cut, expand, or redesign. Drives the agenda for the next gathering.

### Q6. Was the balance of structured sessions vs. free/social time right?
- **Type:** Single choice — Too much structure / About right / Too little structure
- **Goal:** Tunes the single biggest lever on a residential retreat's feel. Informs how tightly to schedule next time.

### Q7. How was the pacing of the days?
- **Type:** Single choice — Too packed / Just right / Too loose
- **Goal:** Separate from Q6 (balance) — this is about intensity/fatigue. Helps decide whether to add buffer or fill gaps.

### Q8. Did the self-organizing "marketplace" format work for deciding what to do?
- **Type:** 1–5, plus optional "why?" (open text)
- **Goal:** The marketplace is a signature, higher-risk design choice. This validates whether to keep relying on it or add more structure/pre-planning.

## Section 3 — Facilitation

### Q9. How well were sessions facilitated overall? ⭐
- **Type:** 1–5
- **Goal:** Headline facilitation quality metric, trackable over time and across facilitator teams.

### Q10. Rate the facilitation on each dimension:
- **Type:** Matrix, 1–5 — rows: clear goals/outcomes · time management · drawing in all voices (not dominated by a few) · keeping sessions on-track
- **Goal:** Turns a vague "facilitation was good/bad" into specific, coachable areas. Tells the team exactly what to improve.

### Q11. What should facilitators start / stop / keep doing?
- **Type:** Open text (three prompts)
- **Goal:** The most actionable qualitative feedback in the survey — concrete behavior changes rather than ratings.

## Section 4 — Outcomes & Impact

> The "so what." The MIRA workshop is a working session, not a conference — so measure outcomes, not just enjoyment. This section is where the *standard* and the *gathering* meet.

### Q12. Did you make new connections or potential collaborations you expect to continue? ⭐
- **Type:** Single choice — Yes, several / A few / Not really
- **Goal:** Network formation is a primary reason to gather people in person. A core ROI metric.

### Q13. Did you leave with a concrete commitment or next step — and how confident are you you'll follow through?
- **Type:** Single choice (Yes / Somewhat / No) + confidence 1–5
- **Goal:** Ties directly to the "road to adoption & commitments" session. The confidence sub-score predicts whether momentum survives after everyone goes home — and flags who/what to follow up with.

### Q14. How much clearer is the MIRA schema / the direction of the standard to you now than before the workshop? ⭐
- **Type:** 1–5
- **Goal:** Measures progress on MIRA's actual intellectual mission — the schema/standard itself — not just the experience of attending. A headline *outcome* metric, trackable each gathering.

## Section 5 — Logistics & Venue

### Q15. Rate the following:
- **Type:** Matrix, 1–5 — rows: accommodation · food · travel/transport logistics · outdoor & social activities (sauna, hikes, cold plunge, etc.)
- **Goal:** Closes the loop on the heavy logistics lift (shuttles, Ubers, dietary planning). Pinpoints where to invest or simplify next time.

### Q16. Was the workshop length (Sun–Thu, ~5 days) right?
- **Type:** Single choice — Too short / Just right / Too long
- **Goal:** Directly informs the format of the next gathering; pairs with the cadence question (Q18).

### Q17. Anything about logistics that created friction for you?
- **Type:** Open text
- **Goal:** Catches specific pain points (a missed shuttle, a dietary gap) that ratings alone won't reveal.

## Section 6 — The Future (of the gathering)

### Q18. What's the ideal time until the next MIRA workshop? ⭐
- **Type:** Single choice — 4 months / 6 months / 12 months / Other ___
- **Goal:** Sets cadence and helps with planning/budgeting. (Original organizer question.)

### Q19. Would you personally like to attend the next MIRA workshop?
- **Type:** Single choice — Definitely / Probably / Depends / No
- **Goal:** Gauges retention and gives a rough headcount for planning. Pairs with Q18.

### Q20. For the next MIRA workshop, what's your location preference?
- **Type:** Single choice — Return to Deerstone / New venue, same region (Ireland/UK/Europe) / New venue, different region or continent / No strong preference
- **Goal:** Decides whether to rebook Deerstone or scout new venues. (Original organizer question, expanded beyond binary.)

### Q21. (Optional) What made the venue work or not work for you?
- **Type:** Open text
- **Goal:** Explains the Q20 vote so the decision isn't made on a number alone.

### Q22. This workshop had ~23 participants. For future gatherings, the ideal size is:
- **Type:** Single choice — Smaller (more intimate) / About the same / Somewhat larger / Much larger
- **Goal:** Probes the core intimacy-vs-reach tradeoff. Anchored to the real number so answers are concrete. (Original organizer question.)

### Q23. How would you like to be involved in [IOSP](https://iosp.science) 2026?
- **Type:** Multi-select — Attend / Help organize or plan / Facilitate a session / Sponsor or help fund / Help recruit participants / Not sure yet / Probably won't participate
- **Goal:** Gauges appetite and recruits volunteers, organizers, and funders for the next IOSP gathering. (Original organizer question, expanded from attend/organize/facilitate to include funding & recruiting.)
- **Build note:** "IOSP" links to <https://iosp.science> so respondents who don't already know it can read what it is.

## Section 7 — Closing

### Q24. Keep / Change / Stop — one of each.
- **Type:** Open text (three prompts: What should we **keep**? What should we **change**? What should we **stop**?)
- **Goal:** The highest-yield open-ended in the survey — forces prioritized, actionable feedback across the whole gathering.

### Q25. Anything we didn't ask that you'd like us to know?
- **Type:** Open text
- **Goal:** Safety net for blind spots in the survey design.

### Q26. (Optional) Your name
- **Type:** Open text
- **Goal:** Lets willing participants attach their name for follow-up (e.g., on commitments from Q13 or volunteering from Q23), while keeping the survey anonymous by default.

## Open design decisions
1. **Delivery:** Build into the existing Next.js + Supabase dashboard (data tied to participants, fully owned) vs. Google Form / Typeform (zero-dev, fast).
2. **Anonymity:** Recommended anonymous + optional name (Q26). Confirm.
3. **Trim:** This is the full menu (28 questions) — longer than you'll likely ship. The irreducible core, if you want a short version: Q1 (overall), Q2 (confidence in MIRA's direction), Q14 (schema progress), Q9 (facilitation), Q12 (connections), Q18 (cadence), and Q24 (keep/change/stop). The Q2/Q2b/Q2c trio (confidence / contribute / advocate) together read the health of the standard and its community — keep all three if MIRA-the-standard is the priority.
